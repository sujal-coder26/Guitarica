Guitar Theory-Scales

A guitar scale is a group of notes that go well together and may be used to compose music. Different guitar scales (for example, major, minor, and pentatonic) employ different note choices and alter the mood of music you may produce. Here are some samples of the various sorts of guitar scales you'll encounter: Major Scale, Minor Scale, Pentatonic Scale, Blues Scale, Modes.
Guitar scales are used to compose music because they help you figure out what notes would sound well together. A scale can inform you which notes will likely work well together and can help you decide out what to play next if you're creating a solo for a tune.
