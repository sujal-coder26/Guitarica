Keys

The key of a song is a quick way to figure out what chords and notes the song is built around. The notes and chords utilized in a song in the key of C will be based on the C Major Scale. The notes and chords of a song in the key of D would be based on the D Major Scale.This can be confusing at first. If a song is in the key of C, it means there are no sharp or flat notes (take a look at the C Major Scale to learn more). But just because the song is in the key of C, it doesnâ€™t mean it must stick to those notes. The song can easily make use of notes and chords outside of the key. At first, this might be perplexing. When a song is written in the key of C, there are no sharp or flat notes (take a look at the C Major Scale to learn more). However, just because the music is in the key of C doesn't imply it has to stay in that key. Outside of the key, the song may easily employ notes and chords.

So, There are two ways of finding the keys of the song. First is by looking at the tabs. Not all songs has tabs. So this is only used when you see the tab chart. In the chart there is a indicator for # and b. So, just by seeing the number of sharp, flats from the chart we can identify the key of the song. If there is only one # symbol in the tab chart then we can say that the key of the song is G major/E minor. If it has 1 flat symbol then the song is in the key of F.
